The biggest mistake in AI visibility strategy in 2026 is treating all AI platforms as one audience.

They're not. Each major AI platform — ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Perplexity, and Claude — uses a distinct retrieval architecture, draws from different source ecosystems, and applies different quality signals when deciding what to cite. A content strategy that earns consistent citations in ChatGPT may do almost nothing for you in Gemini. What Claude rewards, ChatGPT ignores.

The data confirms this isn't a small difference. Research shows that **only 7% of cited sources achieve cross-platform presence across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini simultaneously**, while 71% appear on just one platform. The four platforms agree on which brand to recommend for the same query only 41% of the time.

For agencies managing clients who need AI visibility, this means platform-specific strategy isn't a premium add-on. It's table stakes.

## The Strategic Baseline: What All Platforms Share

Before getting platform-specific, there are three signals that improve citation likelihood across all four platforms — the floor every brand needs in place before any platform-specific work:

1. **Structured data markup** — particularly Article, FAQPage, Organization, and Product schemas. SE Ranking research found ~65% of AI-cited pages include structured data. Wellows' analysis associates it with a 73% citation probability boost across platforms.
2. **Page load speed** — ChatGPT specifically shows a 3× citation advantage for pages with First Contentful Paint under 0.4 seconds vs over 1.13 seconds. Clean technical performance is not optional.
3. **Multi-platform entity presence** — appearing consistently on Wikipedia, G2 or Capterra (for SaaS/software), and major press creates the cross-source verification that all platforms use in Stage 2 authority validation.

These are the baseline. Now here's what differentiates each platform.

## ChatGPT: The Consensus Seeker

**Primary retrieval sources:** Wikipedia (7.8% of citations), Reddit (12%), G2, Capterra, PCMag, TechRadar, academic sources, Bing-indexed content

**Best content formats:** "Best X of 2026" roundup posts, comparison content, fresh news-style content when recency is implied, Wikipedia-style definitional content

**What ChatGPT is doing** when it answers a commercial query is looking for broad consensus — multiple independent sources that agree on the answer. Wikipedia provides definitional authority. Reddit provides community validation. G2 and Capterra provide structured comparative data that ChatGPT can extract cleanly.

For B2B SaaS queries specifically, ChatGPT cites competitor websites 11.1 percentage points more frequently than Google does — it actively values going to branded domains when the query implies product research. It also favours encyclopedic sources (+3.1 points) and academic sources (+1.4 points) compared to traditional search engines.

**The core strategic insight for ChatGPT:** your brand needs multi-source consensus. If you're mentioned positively on Reddit *and* appear in G2 reviews *and* have a clean Wikipedia entity *and* are listed in major software roundups, ChatGPT has the signal density to cite you confidently. Single-source presence — even a great website — is insufficient.

**Technical signal:** pages with First Contentful Paint under 0.4 seconds average 6.7 citations in ChatGPT; pages over 1.13 seconds drop to 2.1 citations. Speed is a real differentiator.

**The content format that over-indexes:** pages explicitly titled as "Best [Category] Tools" or "Top [Category] Platforms for [Use Case]" — aggregative, comparative content that gives ChatGPT the list structure it needs to synthesise a recommendation.

## Gemini: The Authority Inheritor

**Primary retrieval sources:** Google Search index, LinkedIn (B2B queries), YouTube (fastest-growing citation source), Google properties, structured corporate content

**Best content formats:** LinkedIn articles and thought leadership posts, YouTube explainer videos with transcripts, structured blog posts with clear E-E-A-T signals, Google Business Profile data

**What Gemini is doing** differently from every other platform is drawing from the Google ecosystem. It inherits Google's established authority signals — Domain Authority, backlink profiles, E-E-A-T — to a degree that Perplexity and ChatGPT don't. The overlap between Gemini's citations and Google's top-10 organic results is significantly higher than for other AI platforms.

For professional B2B queries, Gemini leans heavily on LinkedIn — company profiles, employee thought leadership posts, and LinkedIn articles. This is largely absent from other platforms' citation sources.

YouTube's citation share grew 34% in six months, and Ahrefs' analysis of 75,000 brands found YouTube brand mentions to be the strongest single correlating factor with AI Overview visibility among all signals tested. Gemini benefits from the fact that YouTube content now includes high-quality transcripts and structured metadata that make video content easily parseable by AI retrieval systems.

**The core strategic insight for Gemini:** traditional SEO legitimacy signals carry over — but the new differentiator is YouTube and LinkedIn presence. Brands that publish regular explainer content on YouTube (with optimised titles, descriptions, and transcripts) and maintain active thought leadership on LinkedIn are positioned for Gemini in a way that no other AI platform rewards.

**Reddit's irrelevance here is real:** Reddit accounts for just 0.1% of Gemini citations. If Gemini is your client's primary target platform, community investment in Reddit delivers near-zero return for Gemini visibility specifically.

## Perplexity: The Freshness Engine

**Primary retrieval sources:** Real-time live web crawl, Reddit (46.7% of top citations), official product documentation, news sites, review platforms (G2, Yelp, Trustpilot), industry blogs updated frequently

**Best content formats:** Answer-first paragraphs with definitive statements, frequently updated content with visible date signals, product documentation pages, press coverage on fast-indexing news outlets

**What Perplexity is doing** that none of the other platforms do is performing real-time retrieval on every single query. There is no training cutoff to worry about — Perplexity is reading the live web when your buyer asks the question. This makes it the fastest platform to respond to new content, and the most sensitive to content freshness signals.

Research from Discoveredlabs found that Perplexity can surface and cite new content within 7–14 days of publication — dramatically faster than ChatGPT's training-based inclusion timelines. Pages updated within the previous three months average nearly double the citation rate of older content.

The Reddit overlap on Perplexity (46.7% of top citations) is extraordinary — nearly 1 in 2 Perplexity answers references a Reddit thread. Combined with Perplexity's real-time retrieval, this creates a specific strategic opportunity: brands that are discussed in recent, active Reddit threads have a path to Perplexity citations within days, not months.

**The core strategic insight for Perplexity:** freshness and community presence are everything. Update core content pages quarterly with new data. Build off-site mentions on third-party review platforms and news sites (Perplexity actively crawls them). And ensure your brand is present in the Reddit communities your buyers use.

**The content format that over-indexes:** answer-first paragraphs with definitive statements. Perplexity is an answer engine — it prefers content that leads with the answer, not a preamble. Content structured as "Question → Direct Answer → Supporting Detail" maps directly to Perplexity's synthesis model.

## Claude: The Transparency Validator

**Primary retrieval sources:** Brave Search index (top 5–10 results), Wikipedia, academic databases, government records, established business directories

**Best content formats:** Structured technical documentation, whitepapers, long-form guides with clear section headers and bullet-pointed definitions, content with explicit limitation/risk sections, FAQ-structured content

**What Claude is doing** is fundamentally different from all the others. Its Constitutional AI framework — built around helpfulness, harmlessness, and honesty — actively shapes what it cites. Pure marketing copy receives a 0.8× citation multiplier (a penalty). Content that acknowledges limitations or risks receives a **1.7× citation boost**.

The factor weighting Claude applies when evaluating sources is specific:
- Entity verification: 30%
- Technical accuracy: 25%
- Traditional database presence (Wikipedia, academic, government): 20%
- Constitutional alignment (honest, balanced, harmless): 15%
- Multimodal support: 10%

Claude uses Brave Search rather than Google or Bing for web retrieval — meaning your Google rankings and Bing optimization have near-zero direct influence on Claude citations. Brave Search has its own crawl index, and submitting your site explicitly to Brave's Web Discovery Project is one of the most high-leverage, lowest-competition tactics for improving Claude visibility.

**The core strategic insight for Claude:** Claude rewards brands that write like subject matter experts with nothing to hide. Technical depth, balanced comparisons that acknowledge competitor strengths, explicit limitation sections, and content cited with sources all improve Claude citation likelihood. Marketing language — particularly benefit-heavy promotional copy — actively reduces it.

**70% of Claude's top citations are verified across multiple authoritative sources** before appearing. Building presence on Wikipedia, traditional databases, and established business directories isn't optional for Claude — it's the prerequisite.

## Key Takeaways

- Only 7% of cited sources achieve presence across all four major AI platforms simultaneously. 71% appear on just one. Platform-agnostic strategies consistently underperform.
- ChatGPT rewards multi-source consensus — Reddit + G2 + Wikipedia + comparison roundups. Fast page load (under 0.4s FCP) gives a 3× citation advantage.
- Gemini draws from the Google ecosystem — E-E-A-T signals, LinkedIn thought leadership, and YouTube content with transcripts. Reddit is near-irrelevant for Gemini.
- Perplexity runs on freshness and real-time community signals — update content quarterly, build off-site review presence, and participate genuinely in relevant Reddit communities.
- Claude rewards transparency and technical depth via Constitutional AI — limitation sections earn a 1.7× citation boost; pure marketing copy is actively penalised. Register on Brave Search's index.

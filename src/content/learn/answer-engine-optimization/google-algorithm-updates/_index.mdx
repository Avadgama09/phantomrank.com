Google algorithm updates have shaped SEO strategy for over two decades. Every confirmed update adjusts how Google evaluates content, ranks pages, and presents results. For agencies doing [answer engine optimization](/learn/answer-engine-optimization/what-is-aeo), these updates matter doubly: they affect both traditional organic rankings and the AI systems that select sources for AI Overviews, featured snippets, and other answer surfaces.

Understanding how updates work—what Google targets, how ranking systems shift, and what patterns emerge—helps agencies build strategies that hold up through volatility rather than chasing recovery after every rollout.

## How Google Algorithm Updates Work

Google's search algorithm is not a single system. It is a collection of interconnected ranking systems that evaluate content quality, relevance, authority, user satisfaction, and technical performance. Algorithm updates modify one or more of these systems.

### Core updates vs. targeted updates

**Core updates** are broad changes to Google's overall ranking systems. They re-evaluate how all signals are weighted relative to each other, which can cause significant ranking fluctuations across many industries simultaneously. Core updates do not target specific sites or issues. They refine how Google compares content quality across the web.

**Targeted updates** address specific aspects of search. Spam updates crack down on manipulative practices. Link spam updates refine how backlinks are evaluated. Helpful content updates (now folded into core systems) assess whether content is created primarily for people or for search engines. These updates have narrower scope but can hit affected sites hard.

**Minor updates** happen continuously. Google makes thousands of changes to its systems each year, most of which are not formally announced. These incremental adjustments cause the background volatility that SEOs observe between confirmed updates.

### Why updates cause ranking fluctuations

When Google recalibrates how it weighs signals, pages that previously benefited from a particular signal balance may lose ground to pages that perform better under the new balance. This is not a penalty. It is a re-evaluation.

A page that ranked well because of strong backlinks might lose position if the update increases the weight of content freshness or semantic depth. Conversely, a page with exceptional content quality might gain position even if its backlink profile is modest.

The fluctuation period typically lasts days to weeks. Google recommends waiting at least a full week after an update completes before analyzing impact in Search Console. Premature analysis during the rollout period produces misleading data because rankings are still settling.

## Recent Google Algorithm Updates: 2024-2025

The update cadence over the past two years reveals clear patterns about where Google is heading. These patterns directly inform AEO strategy.

### 2024 Updates

**March 2024 Core Update** was one of the most significant updates in recent memory. It launched on March 5 and took 45 days to complete—one of the longest rollouts on record. Multiple core systems were updated simultaneously, causing sustained ranking fluctuations.

Key impacts included a crackdown on "parasite SEO" where reputable sites hosted low-quality third-party content, enhanced evaluation of content originality and usefulness, and refined spam detection. Google stated this update reduced unhelpful content in search by 45%.

This update also formally incorporated the Helpful Content system into the broader core ranking system. That means "helpful content" is no longer a separate ranking signal—it is integrated into how Google evaluates everything.

**November 2024 Core Update** launched on November 11 and completed on December 5. It was smaller in scope, focused on making results more relevant and helpful. SERP volatility was moderate.

**December 2024 Core Update** followed just days later on December 12, completing by December 18. This rapid succession surprised many, but Google explained it as a continuation of work started with the November update. A December 2024 spam update also rolled out on December 19, targeting manipulative ranking practices.

### 2025 Updates

Google confirmed four algorithm updates in 2025—three core updates and one spam update. This is fewer than prior years (seven confirmed in 2024, nine in 2023), but the reduced announcement cadence does not mean less volatility. Google has indicated it may confirm fewer updates going forward while continuing to make significant changes.

**March 2025 Core Update** started rolling out on March 13 and completed on March 27. Google described it as a "regular update designed to better surface relevant, satisfying content for searchers from all types of sites." Google also signaled ongoing work to "surface more content from creators through a series of improvements throughout this year."

**June 2025 Core Update** continued the pattern of evaluating content quality and depth, rewarding expertise and originality, and devaluing low-quality or overly optimized content.

**August 2025 Spam Update** targeted manipulative ranking practices specifically. This was the only non-core confirmed update of the year.

**December 2025 Core Update** launched on December 11 and completed on December 29. Like other 2025 core updates, it focused on broad improvements to ranking systems rather than targeting specific sites or issues. Ranking and traffic fluctuations were notable during the rollout period.

### The pattern emerging from recent updates

Several consistent themes run through the 2024 and 2025 update cycles:

- **Stronger evaluation of content quality and depth.** Google is getting better at distinguishing genuinely expert content from surface-level coverage. Depth without substance (word count padding, keyword repetition) is less effective than it was.
- **Continued devaluation of low-quality and manipulative content.** Sites relying on scaled AI-generated content, parasite SEO, or link manipulation continue to lose ground.
- **Increased reward for originality and creator content.** Google explicitly mentioned surfacing more content from creators. Original research, unique perspectives, and first-hand experience are gaining weight.
- **Fewer confirmed updates does not mean fewer changes.** Google is shifting toward less frequent announcements while making equally significant changes. Agencies cannot afford to monitor only during confirmed update windows.

## How Algorithm Updates Affect Answer Engine Visibility

Algorithm updates do not just change organic rankings. They change the source pool that AI Overviews and other answer systems draw from. When rankings shift, AI citation patterns often shift with them.

### Direct ranking impact

When a core update changes which pages rank for a given query, the AI Overview for that query may change its cited sources. A page that loses significant organic position may also lose its AI Overview citation. A page that gains position may become newly eligible for citation.

This relationship is weakening, however. Recent research shows the overlap between top-10 organic rankings and AI Overview citations has declined from roughly 76% to somewhere between 17% and 38%. Google's AI is increasingly making independent source selections based on content quality, structure, and authority—not just ranking position.

### Content quality re-evaluation

Core updates that raise the bar for content quality have a direct effect on which pages AI systems trust enough to cite. If an update devalues thin, unoriginal content across traditional rankings, those same pages become less likely to appear in AI Overviews.

Conversely, pages that gain organic visibility because an update rewards their content quality become stronger AI citation candidates. The quality signals Google uses for traditional ranking and AI source selection overlap substantially.

### Freshness and authority recalibration

Updates sometimes adjust how heavily freshness and authority are weighted. An update that increases the importance of content recency gives regularly updated pages an advantage in both rankings and AI citations. An update that strengthens domain authority signals favors established publishers over newer sources.

These shifts ripple through AI Overview source selection because the AI's evaluation systems share infrastructure with Google's broader ranking systems.

## How to Build Update-Resilient AEO Strategy

The most effective response to algorithm updates is not reactive recovery. It is building content and technical infrastructure that remains strong regardless of how individual signals get reweighted.

### Focus on content quality as the constant

Every major algorithm update over the past several years has rewarded content quality, expertise, and originality. This trend is not going to reverse. Content that demonstrates genuine expertise, provides specific and sourced information, and offers original value will perform well through updates.

That means your AEO work should prioritize substance over format tricks. Atomic answers, question-based headings, and structured formats only work when the underlying content is genuinely authoritative. Structure without substance is fragile. Substance with good structure is resilient.

### Maintain freshness systematically

Stale content is vulnerable during every update cycle. When Google recalibrates quality signals, outdated content often loses ground to fresher alternatives. Build a content refresh calendar that updates priority pages quarterly for fast-moving topics and biannually for stable topics.

Track which pages are due for refresh and ensure statistics, examples, and market context are current before each major update cycle. Proactive freshness is easier than reactive recovery.

### Diversify traffic sources beyond organic

Pages that depend entirely on one ranking position for one keyword are fragile. A single update can eliminate that position. Diversify by targeting multiple related keywords, building [featured snippet](/learn/answer-engine-optimization/featured-snippet-optimization) positions, earning AI Overview citations, and developing content clusters that provide multiple entry points.

When one pathway shifts during an update, others may hold steady or improve. Diversification reduces the impact of any single ranking change.

### Build genuine E-E-A-T over time

Authority and trust signals take time to build but provide lasting resilience. Strong author credentials, established domain authority, consistent publication history, and verified organizational identity all create trust signals that algorithm updates reward rather than undermine.

Agencies that invest in long-term E-E-A-T building protect their clients from the worst update impacts. Sites with genuine authority rarely experience catastrophic drops during core updates because their quality signals are robust across multiple dimensions.

## What to Do When an Update Hits

Despite best practices, ranking fluctuations during updates are normal. Here is a practical response framework for agencies.

### During the rollout: do not panic

Algorithm updates take days to weeks to complete. Rankings fluctuate throughout the rollout as different systems are updated. Reacting to mid-rollout data is premature and often misleading.

Monitor for awareness, but avoid making content changes during the rollout period. Changes made during a rollout get tangled with the update itself, making it impossible to isolate what caused any given shift.

### After completion: analyze systematically

Wait at least one full week after the update completes. Then compare performance in Search Console across a meaningful time range. Google recommends comparing the period after the update to the period before it, using multi-week windows.

Identify which pages gained or lost visibility. Look for patterns:

- Did losses concentrate in a specific content type or topic area?
- Do affected pages share common characteristics—age, word count, structure, link profile?
- Did competitors who gained visibility have noticeably different content qualities?

These patterns reveal what the update likely adjusted in signal weighting.

### Prioritize recovery actions

If specific pages lost significant visibility, evaluate them against the patterns you identified. Common recovery actions include:

- **Improving content depth and originality.** If losses concentrated on thinner content, invest in making those pages genuinely more comprehensive and expert.
- **Updating stale information.** If older content dropped while newer content gained, freshness may have been reweighted. Prioritize updating critical pages.
- **Strengthening E-E-A-T signals.** If losses affected YMYL content or pages with weak authority markers, add author credentials, cite authoritative sources, and improve organizational transparency.
- **Cleaning technical issues.** If losses correlate with poor page speed, mobile experience problems, or crawl issues, address technical debt.
- **Removing low-quality content.** If the update targeted thin or unhelpful content, auditing and removing or consolidating weak pages can improve site-wide quality signals.

### Document and learn

Record what happened during each update: which pages were affected, what recovery actions you took, what worked. This institutional knowledge becomes increasingly valuable over time. Patterns across multiple update cycles reveal your client's specific vulnerability areas and inform preventive strategy.

## Algorithm Updates and the Future of AEO

Google's algorithm updates are converging on a clear direction: search is becoming more AI-integrated, more quality-focused, and more answer-oriented.

### AI integration in ranking systems

Google's ranking systems and AI systems share infrastructure. As AI Overviews expand across more query types, the distinction between "ranking well" and "getting cited by AI" will continue to blur. Algorithm updates will increasingly affect both organic positions and AI citation patterns simultaneously.

This convergence means [AEO strategy](/learn/answer-engine-optimization/what-is-aeo) and traditional SEO strategy are becoming more aligned, not less. The fundamentals that protect rankings during updates—quality content, strong authority, technical accessibility, freshness—are the same fundamentals that earn AI citations.

### Fewer confirmed updates, more continuous evolution

Google is moving toward confirming fewer updates while making continuous changes. This means agencies cannot rely on update announcements as triggers for review. Instead, build continuous monitoring into your workflow. Track rankings, AI citations, and answer surface visibility weekly, not just when Google announces a change.

### Quality as the durable strategy

Every algorithm update in the past five years has rewarded content quality, expertise, and user value. This trajectory is not going to reverse. Agencies that build their AEO programs around genuine quality—rather than tactical manipulation of specific ranking factors—will navigate updates with less disruption and recover faster when fluctuations occur.

The most update-resilient strategy is also the best AEO strategy: create genuinely authoritative, well-structured, current, and trustworthy content that answers real questions clearly. That alignment is not a coincidence. It reflects where Google is heading.
